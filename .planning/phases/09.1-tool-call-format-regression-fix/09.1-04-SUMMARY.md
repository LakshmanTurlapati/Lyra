## Deviations from Plan

### Below-Target Counts

The plan targeted ~25,000 total curated samples with minimums of 10K tool-calling, 2K code, 2K knowledge, and 14K total. Actual results:

- **Tool-calling: 11,992** -- PASSES 10K minimum (52% retention)
- **Code: 649** -- BELOW 2K minimum (13.8% retention due to template-pool exhaustion)
- **Knowledge: 651** -- BELOW 2K minimum (14.1% retention due to template-pool exhaustion)
- **Total: 13,292** -- BELOW 14K minimum by 708 samples (95% of target)

**Root cause:** RESEARCH.md Pitfall 1 predicted this -- template-based generation with finite diversity pools (192 topics for knowledge, limited language-specific code pools) produces high near-duplicate rates at scale. The dedup is correctly identifying duplicates. The code domain had 86% removal at dedup stage + further style filtering; knowledge had 86% removal at dedup.

**Impact:** The training dataset has 13,292 samples instead of 25,000. The tool-calling domain (primary target for the regression fix) is well-represented at 11,992 (above the 10K minimum). Code and knowledge domains are smaller than hoped but still represent 3.7x and 3.7x scale-up from the original 175-sample counts (after curation). The domain ratio is heavily skewed toward tool-calling (90.2% vs 68% target).

**Plan says to continue:** The plan explicitly states "If retention drops below 50% for any domain, log a warning but continue -- the dedup is doing its job removing near-duplicates from template-based generation."

## Issues Encountered
- Tool-calling O(n^2) dedup on 23,050 samples took ~28 minutes (265M pairwise comparisons). The dedup.py docstring notes "At 5K samples... completes in seconds. For >10K samples, consider MinHash LSH." Future scaling beyond 25K would benefit from approximate dedup.
